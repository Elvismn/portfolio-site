My Journey

I strongly believe that with focus, commitment, and determination, anyone can achieve their goals. This belief has guided me from the hills of Ol-joro-orok to the halls of the Technical University of Kenya, shaping every step of my journey.

Growing up, I learned that discipline and hard work open doors. At Chavakali High School, I channeled this mindset into both academics and extracurriculars—excelling in chess and hockey while maintaining the discipline that earned me an A- (76 points). These experiences taught me that success isn't just about individual achievement; it's about teamwork, strategy, and resilience.

My journey took a transformative turn when I joined the Education for All Children (EFAC) program. More than financial support, EFAC provided mentorship, career guidance, and a community of driven scholars who push each other to excel. This experience ignited my passion for leadership and service.

Today, as a Mechanical Engineering student at the Technical University of Kenya, I'm bridging the gap between traditional engineering and modern technology. My software development skills—honed through programs like ALX Africa and Power Learn Project—allow me to approach problems holistically, combining physical engineering with digital solutions. I'm particularly passionate about automation, robotics, and sustainable design.

Beyond academics, I'm building connections through communities like Kiboko DAO and Aspire Leaders Program, where I've learned from Harvard faculty and connected with global peers. These experiences continue to shape my vision: to create meaningful impact at the intersection of engineering, technology, and community development.
